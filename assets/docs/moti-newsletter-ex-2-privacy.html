Innovation Project Spotlight

During our most recent Smart City and Services Improvement Committee meeting on December 6, we gave an update on our ongoing efforts to craft a city-wide Privacy Policy. Though far from being done, we wanted to be transparent and open as to how we are thinking about these issues. See below from a Draft of our Digital Privacy Principles, which will ultimately inform our city-wide Privacy Policy, and read below to understand how we got here, and how we are planning to move forward in this area:

Our Draft Privacy Principles, presented to Council at the Dec 6th Smart Cities & Services Improvement Committee.

In May 2018, the first City Privacy Working Group was established. Working with the Harvard CyberLaw Clinic, we were able to benchmark and survey how other leading peer-cities have been managing the crafting of their digital privacy policies, helping us learn from the missteps and successes of others. To further help us in refining and crafting our Privacy Principles, in October we onboarded CivicMakers, an innovative public/community engagement firm that is helping frame, facilitate and inform our City‚Äôs strategy for engaging residents around issues of digital and personal privacy."

Since Digital Privacy is a very important issue to our community, we have been working with them and with City employees to engage a variety of stakeholders to help co-craft and inform our Privacy Principles. This has meant creating a Privacy Advisory Task Force, which first met at the end of November, composed of advisors from the non-profit, law enforcement, government and tech industries. They provide external subject-matter advice and expertise on issues related to digital privacy. We have also hosted our first community workshop on privacy in early December. With a positive first reception, we are looking forward to hosting more of these community meetings in different areas of the city, paying special attention to make these accessible to our residents who might prefer languages other than English, and hosting them during times of the day that are easier to attend for all our residents. Special thanks to Liam Crawford, Broadband and Privacy Policy Analyst for the City Manager‚Äôs Office, and Rob Lloyd, our Chief Information Officer, for all their work in this space!
